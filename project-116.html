Rehabilitation Recreation Center

"Recreation and Creative Arts Therapists provide non-traditional and innovative modalities that assist Veterans in reaching their treatment goals and obtaining improved health and quality of life."
Caroline Wyman, Chief, Recreation Therapy Service at VAPAHCS

Project Details
  • Client: Department of Veterans Affairs
  • Location: Menlo Park, CA
  • Size: 4.4 acres
  • Construction Cost: $8,700,000

This project will decommission and raze seismically and/or functionally-deficient buildings, including the 80,000 square foot Building 323 and a number of other smaller buildings, located in VA Menlo Park division campus. The demolition of these existing buildings will provide open space for a new outdoor sports field facility, pavilion, clubhouse, and a storage shed.

The new clubhouse will be a 5,000 gross square foot building consisting of a lounge with warming pantry, sports equipment storage and check out office, administrative office, locker rooms and restrooms. Spectators may access a partial roof viewing area to achieve views over the sports fields.

The new Clubhouse building is intended to incorporate many of the recommended sustainable design strategies from the USGBC, including:

  • Pervious paving and vegetation for the site to minimize storm water runoff into the sewage system.
  • Views and natural daylighting of occupied spaces.
  • Mechanical systems and design features to attenuate sound and vibration.
  • Building materials with recycled contenct where available. Goal of 10% of locally manufactured building materials. Goal of 50% of wood based products will be FSC certified. Elimination of Mercury, Lead and Cadmium from building materials and control systems.
  • Use of low or no VOC paints, sealants, adhesives, composite wood products, insulation, carpet systems, furniture furnishings, and exterior applied products such as roofing and waterproofing materials.

The new outdoor recreational facility consists of a softball field, a multipurpose field, a basketball court, a tennis court, two bocce ball courts, two horseshoe courts, two shuffleboard courts, two putting greens and par-course equipment area.

These facilities will enable the Recreation Therapy group to offer enhanced and expanded treatment programs to veterans. As of July 2015, this project is in the early stages of construction.
